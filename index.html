wisdom on boredom
Gurudev Sri Sri Ravi Shankar

The ocean waves without pause and never gets bored. Birds sing the same song each morning, all their lives. Only humans get bored — because of memory. And this memory is both a gift and a burden.

Boredom as a blessing — it pushes you to seek something new, something deeper. It turns you into a seeker. It pulls you away from the routine and wakes you up.

Boredom as a curse — left unchecked, it leads to frustration and depression. The mind wanders aimlessly, unable to settle or enjoy anything.

The bridge — meditation connects the head and heart. Sitting with boredom, without escaping it, opens a doorway into deep stillness. The Self is ever new, ever fresh — boredom is just the surface layer.

"Boredom is a blessing when it leads you to wisdom. A curse when it leads you to frustration and depression."

— Gurudev Sri Sri Ravi Shankar

how to overcome it
1
Find a bigger purpose When your heart beats for something beyond yourself — a cause, a person, a craft — boredom quietly disappears. Commitment is the antidote.
2
Meditate through it Instead of escaping boredom, sit with it. Practice — abhyasa — penetrates boredom and dissolves it. Root it out through continued, deep meditation.
3
Love what you repeat Repetition without love creates boredom. Repetition with love creates devotion. The Self is love — and love never bores.
